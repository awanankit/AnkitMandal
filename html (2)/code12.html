Khudiram Bose

Khudiram Bose (1889–1908) was an Indian revolutionary and one of the youngest martyrs in the Indian independence movement. He is best known for his role in the Muzaffarpur bombing in 1908, where he attempted to assassinate a British judge but mistakenly killed two women instead. Bose was arrested, tried, and hanged at the age of 18. His sacrifice inspired many in the fight against British colonial rule in India.

Born in Midnapore, Bengal, Bose joined the Anushilan Samiti, a revolutionary group. His act of bravery made him a symbol of youthful rebellion and patriotism.
