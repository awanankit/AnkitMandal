Biography of Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah

Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah (1733–1757) was the last independent Nawab of Bengal, Bihar, and Orissa. He ascended to the throne in 1756 after the death of his grandfather, Alivardi Khan.

Known for his military campaigns, he is most famous for his conflict with the British East India Company, culminating in the Battle of Plassey in 1757, where he was defeated by Robert Clive. This event marked the beginning of British colonial dominance in India.

Siraj-ud-Daulah was captured and executed shortly after the battle. His reign was marked by efforts to curb British influence in Bengal, but internal betrayals and alliances led to his downfall.
