Mughal Emperor Akbar (1542-1605)

Quick Facts

  • Full Name: Jalal-ud-din Muhammad Akbar
  • Reign: 1556 – 1605 (49 years)
  • Born: October 15, 1542, Umerkot
  • Died: October 27, 1605, Agra
  • Title: "Akbar the Great"

Major Achievements

  • Expanded the Mughal Empire to include most of the Indian subcontinent
  • Established the policy of religious tolerance (Din-i-Ilahi)
  • Built the city of Fatehpur Sikri
  • Created a efficient administrative system
  • Promoted art, culture, and literature
  • Encouraged trade and commerce

Notable Buildings

  • Taj Mahal (completed by his son)
  • Fatehpur Sikri
  • Red Fort (Agra)
  • Humayun's Tomb

Legacy

Akbar is considered the greatest Mughal emperor. His policy of religious tolerance, administrative reforms, and patronage of the arts made him one of the most respected rulers in Indian history.
