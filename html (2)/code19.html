Rani Lakshmi Bai

[Image of Rani Lakshmi Bai]

Rani Lakshmi Bai (1828-1858) was the Queen of the princely state of Jhansi in North India. She became a symbol of resistance against British colonial rule during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. Born as Manikarnika Tambe, she was known for her courage and warrior skills.

Quick Facts:

  • Born: 19 November 1828, Varanasi
  • Died: 18 June 1858, Gwalior
  • Nickname: Jhansi Ki Rani
  • Weapon: Two swords, bow and arrows
  • Famous for: Fighting on horseback with two swords

She refused to surrender Jhansi to the British and led her forces in battle. She fought bravely until she was killed in battle at Gwalior. Her bravery and sacrifice made her a national hero and an icon of Indian independence.
