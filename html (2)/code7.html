Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863–1902) was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, and spiritual leader. Born as Narendra Nath Datta in Kolkata, he became a key disciple of Ramakrishna Paramahamsa. He is best known for his inspiring speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he introduced Hinduism to the Western world. Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Mission and emphasized the importance of education, self-reliance, and service to humanity.

His teachings promote the idea that all religions lead to the same truth and encourage personal spiritual growth.
