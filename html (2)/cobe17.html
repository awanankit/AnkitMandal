The Story of Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh (1907-1931)

Indian Revolutionary | Freedom Fighter | Martyr

Early Life

Bhagat Singh was born on September 28, 1907, in a Sikh family in the village of Khatkar Kalan, in the Lyallpur district of Punjab (now Pakistan). He was born to Sardar Kishan Singh and Vidya Devi. His family was deeply involved in the freedom struggle - his father, uncle, and elder brother were all freedom fighters.

Early Influences

From a young age, Bhagat Singh was influenced by the ideals of revolution and patriotism. He was deeply inspired by the sacrifice of freedom fighters like Bhai Phera Singh and the teachings of Swami Vivekananda. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919 left a lasting impact on young Bhagat Singh.

The Revolutionary Journey

1920: Bhagat Singh joined the Non-Cooperation Movement at the age of just 13. He was inspired by Mahatma Gandhi but later became disillusioned with the non-violent approach.
1928: He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA). The same year, Lala Lajpat Rai died after being beaten by police during a protest, which deeply affected Bhagat Singh.
December 1928: Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Batukeshwar Dutt, assassinated Assistant Superintendent of Police J.P. Saunders to avenge Lala Lajpat Rai's death.
April 1929: Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly to protest against the Public Safety Bill and Trade Disputes Bill. They deliberately did not escape to make their point heard.

Imprisonment and Trial

After his arrest, Bhagat Singh became a symbol of resistance. During his imprisonment, he used the court as a platform to spread revolutionary ideas. He wrote extensively and even went on a hunger strike demanding better conditions for political prisoners.

"They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they cannot crush my spirit."
— Bhagat Singh

Martyrdom

On March 23, 1931, at just 23 years of age, Bhagat Singh was hanged along with Rajguru and Sukhdev in the Lahore Central Jail. Their execution sparked massive protests across India. They were cremated at the Mogi Hills (now called Bhagat Singh Nagar) in Lahore.

Legacy

Bhagat Singh's sacrifice inspired millions of Indians in their fight for freedom. He remains one of the most influential and revered revolutionaries in Indian history. His courage, patriotism, and revolutionary spirit continue to inspire generations of Indians.

"In a way, I am quite sure that you might have heard the name of my祖国 (motherland). It is a matter of shame that we have to beg for our freedom. We have shed the choicest blood of our generation. We are a nation of slaves."
— Bhagat Singh
