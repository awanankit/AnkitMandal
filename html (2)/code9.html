Shah Jahan

Shah Jahan (born Prince Khurram; 5 January 1592 – 22 January 1666) was the fifth Mughal emperor of India, reigning from 1628 to 1658. He is best known for commissioning the Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, as a mausoleum for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

During his reign, the Mughal Empire reached its zenith in terms of architecture, art, and culture. Shah Jahan was a patron of the arts and oversaw the construction of many magnificent buildings, including the Red Fort in Delhi and the Jama Masjid.

He was deposed by his son Aurangzeb in 1658 and spent the rest of his life imprisoned in the Agra Fort, where he could see the Taj Mahal from his window.
